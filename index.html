// About the Project

WHY DO THE
LIGHTS GO OUT?

Power Breakers dives deep into historical U.S. power outage records to identify patterns, root causes, and vulnerable regions — using machine learning to turn raw grid failure data into actionable insights.

Through exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, and predictive modeling, our team examined decades of outage events to answer key questions: Which regions suffer most? What triggers large-scale failures? How long do outages typically last?

The result is a comprehensive data-driven study with visualizations and models that illuminate the hidden dynamics of America's electrical grid.

// Key Analysis Areas

WHAT WE
DISCOVERED

🗺️
Geographic Patterns
Mapped outage hotspots across U.S. regions to identify which states and climate zones experience the highest frequency of grid failures.
⏱️
Temporal Trends
Analyzed seasonal, monthly, and hour-of-day patterns to uncover when the grid is most vulnerable and outages are most likely to strike.
⚡
Cause Classification
Used ML classifiers to categorize outage causes — severe weather, equipment failure, intentional attacks, and more — with high accuracy.
📊
Duration Prediction
Built regression models to predict outage duration based on cause, location, season, and infrastructure variables.
🔍
Impact Analysis
Quantified how many customers and megawatts are affected across different outage categories and geographic areas.
📈
Rich Visualizations
Produced detailed charts, heatmaps, and distribution plots using Matplotlib and Seaborn to communicate findings clearly.
